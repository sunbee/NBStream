Assessment:


King Janaka had a dream in which his kingdom was beset by natural disaster. He was scavenging for scraps of food and found a piece of roti. The custom at the time was that a householder would not sit down to eat in the presence of a hungry person until that person was first fed. Fearful of losing his sole scrap of food, Janaka hid the roti under his clothes and sought a lonely and desolate place to sit down and eat. Even then, an eagle swooped down upon him and snatched the roti just as he was about to eat. Desperate, he cried out loud in his dream and woke up to find himself in his royal court, surrounded by courtiers. Being a thoughtful person, Janaka wondered whether he had woken up from a frightening dream into pleasant reality or escaped from harsh reality into a pleasant dream.
